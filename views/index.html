About

GoodHacks is Stanford's first annual hackathon designed to promote social good. It was created by the students, for the students. Whether you're a student, a mover, a non-profit organization or a corporation, GoodHacks has something to offer you.

FAQ

I have never been to hackathon, is that OK?

Yes, GoodHacks welcomes participants of all skill levels, backgrounds and walks of life. Hackathons are a great way to learn about many things whether they be skills, technologies or the world of nonprofits. We will also have mentors available on-site to help you figure out how to make your wildest ideas a reality.

I'm not a Stanford student, can I still participate?

Goodhacks is open to any and all university students interested in hacking on cool and impactful things. After all, our goal is to catalyze as much social good as we can.

Can I still come if I don't have a team?

Definitely! Plenty of people come without teammates. We will release a portal teammate search shortly before the event to help people with similar ideas and aspirations to come together. We will also have an hour and a half set aside before the hacking starts for people to find a team.

What does it cost to attend?

We're pleased to announce that GoodHacks is completely free! Just bring your time, creativity and inspiration.

What should I bring to GoodHacks?

All you need is a computer, chargers and a sleeping bag. We'll take care of everything else.

What's the max team size?

We're capping the team sizes at four. However, if you have a pressing reason to go beyond four, contact us to request an exception.
